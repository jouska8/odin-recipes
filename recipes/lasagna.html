Lasagna

Description

Lasagne is a traditional Italian dish consisting of wide, flat pasta sheets layered with various fillings. It typically includes alternating layers of pasta, meat sauce (such as Bolognese), béchamel (a creamy white sauce), and cheese, particularly ricotta, mozzarella, and Parmesan. The dish is baked until golden and bubbling, creating a hearty and flavorful meal with a rich blend of textures. Lasagne is often served as a comfort food, enjoyed in households and Italian restaurants worldwide.

Ingredients

For the Meat Sauce (Bolognese)

  • 450g ground beef
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1tsp dried oregano
  • 1tsp dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil

For the Béchamel Sauce:

  • 4 tbsp butter
  • 4 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups milk (whole milk preferred)
  • Pinch of nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Pasta:

  • 12-15 lasagne sheets

Steps

  1. Sauté onion and garlic, add ground beef, and brown.
  2. Add tomato paste, crushed tomatoes, oregano, basil, and simmer for 20–30 minutes.
  3. Melt butter, whisk in flour to form a roux.
  4. Slowly add milk, whisk until thickened. Season with salt, pepper, and nutmeg.
  5. Mix ricotta with a beaten egg, salt, and pepper.
  6. Boil lasagne sheets until al dente, then drain and rinse with cold water.
  7. Layer meat sauce, pasta, béchamel, ricotta, and mozzarella in a baking dish.
  8. Repeat layers and finish with cheese.
  9. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 30–40 minutes until golden and bubbling. Let rest before serving.
